Fambit
Fambit is an open browser extension for appreciating content via Bitcoin microdonations

How it works

  • 1
    Send Bitcoin to Fambit wallet
  • 2
    Visit websites like normal
  • 3
    Fambit automatically donates

As a website owner

To receive Fambit donations, add to each of your HTML pages the Bitcoin-recipient meta tag, where value is your Bitcoin address: 

<html>
<head>
    ...
    <meta name="Bitcoin-recipient"
        value="A245345BC523523FE385">
</head>
<body>
    ...
</body>
</html>

Promotes decentralization

  • Donates to every site: so, even small, single-purpose sites will be funded.
  • Author automatically receives Bitcoin, no centralized collection (Brave[0]).
  • Fambit doesn't take a "cut" of donations, unlike other solutions (Brave[1], SatoshiPay[2]).

More friendly than ads, more flexible than crowdfunding

  • Unlike advertisements, absolutely no user tracking[3]
  • Currently, crowdfunding is more about marketing skill and existing reputation, since the donations are set up by users in advance. For budding creators, they don't have the option of monetizing their first content, regardless of quality. With Fambit, donations will properly scale as more users enjoy the content.

FAQ

  • Q: How much is donated each time I visit a site?
  • A: 0.01% of the money currently inside the Fambit "pool" of Bitcoin
  • Q: How often do I need to re-fill Fambit with Bitcoin? That would be a hassle, and I just wanna surf the web like it's 1999, baby
  • A: Since Fambit sends a percentage of the current funds, that means that, over time, it sends less Bitcoin. Though this means that donations decrease in amount, it means that you - as a user - refill the donation pool on your own terms, not when it runs out of funds.
  • Q: What if a site doesn't support Fambit?
  • A: No donation is made. It would be neat-o if that site accepts donations in the future.
  • Q: Does Fambit take a cut of donation money?
  • A: Nuh-uh. We Fambit guys don't profit from this. In fact, Fambit is Open Source!
  • Q: Who owns the money I put into Fambit?
  • A: You do, the Bitcoin wallet information is on your computer and not known by anyone except you.
  • Q: Why is it called "Fambit"?
  • A: This project is an initial attempt in the "open micro-donation" domain: a "gambit". It transfers Bitcoin among yo fams
